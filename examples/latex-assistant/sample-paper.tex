\section{Special Relativity}

The speed of light in vacuum is $c = 3 \times 10^9 \; \text{m/s}$.

% ERROR: c = 3 x 10^8 m/s, not 10^9

The relativistic energy of a particle with rest mass $m$ and momentum $p$ is:
\begin{equation}
    E^2 = (pc)^2 + (mc^2)^2
\end{equation}

The Lorentz factor is:
\begin{equation}
    \gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 + v^2/c^2}}
\end{equation}

% ERROR: should be 1 - v^2/c^2, not 1 + v^2/c^2

\section{Electromagnetism}

Maxwell's equations in differential form (SI units) include Gauss's law:
\begin{equation}
    \nabla \cdot \mathbf{E} = \frac{\rho}{\epsilon_0}
\end{equation}

and Faraday's law:
\begin{equation}
    \nabla \times \mathbf{E} = \frac{\partial \mathbf{B}}{\partial t}
\end{equation}

% ERROR: missing negative sign, should be -dB/dt

\section{Quantum Mechanics}

The time-independent Schr\"{o}dinger equation for a particle in a potential $V(x)$ is:
\begin{equation}
    -\frac{\hbar^2}{2m} \frac{d^2 \psi}{dx^2} + V(x)\psi = E\psi
\end{equation}

The de Broglie wavelength is $\lambda = h/p$, where $h$ is Planck's constant.

The energy of a photon is $E = h\nu$.

% ERROR: by the product rule, f'(x) = 3x^2 e^x + x^3 e^x = (3x^2 + x^3)e^x
